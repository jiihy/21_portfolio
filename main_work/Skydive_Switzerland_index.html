About this Program

This 70-minute long airplane flight will let you discover the very best of what Swiss and French Alps have to offer. After take off, the airplane first takes you over to the world famous trio – the Eiger, Mönch and Jungfrau.

You will then follow the rugged cliffs and ice fields reaching the UNESCO world heritage site Aletsch Glacier, which is the largest glacier in the Alps. The view of the imposing peaks and glacier is breathtaking.

We will next fly over the Valais canton towards the iconic Matterhorn which you can see from afar. As you fly past the Matterhorn, you will have plenty of time to admire its uniqueness and maybe even spot climbers approaching the summit.

To top off the flight, you will continue to the highest peak in the Alps, the majestic Mont Blanc, which rises 4,808 m (15,774 ft) above sea level. From there, you will fly over Chamonix, Martigny and Simme valley to land back in Airport Reichenbach. Airplane scenic flights are available upon request, please contact us directly. If you are less than three people, Scenic Air will be happy to help you find more participants for the flight.

About this Program

Helicopter skydiving over Lauterbrunnen valley is the best way to get up close to the Swiss Alps. An experience you will never forget! With Skydive Switzerland, you can enjoy three activities within one during your helicopter skydive.

Your adventure will start with an 8 minute scenic flight as the helicopter climbs to altitude. Enjoy the views of Lauterbrunnen Valley and the incredible Swiss Alps, including the famous peaks Eiger, Mönch and Jungfrau.

The flight is followed by a 45-second adrenaline-pumping freefall from the altitude of 4,000m (13,000 ft). Feel the ground rushing towards you at 200 km/h (120 mph)!

Once the parachute is deployed, take a deep breath and enjoy the 5-7 minute canopy ride which will take you safely back to the ground. Come summer or winter, we operate daily all year round (except for some national holidays). Get in touch with us to arrange your adventure!

About this Program

Due to Coronavirus restrictions, no tandem jumps available until March 22. The ultimate bucket list adventure, airplane skydiving, is the best way to get up close to the Swiss Alps. An experience you will never forget! With Skydive Switzerland, you can enjoy three activities within one.

Your adventure will start with an approximately 15-minute scenic flight as the jump plane climbs to altitude. Enjoy the views of Interlaken’s two lakes and the incredible Swiss Alps, including the famous peaks of Eiger, Mönch and Jungfrau.

The flight is followed by a 45-second adrenaline-pumping freefall from the altitude of 4,000 m (13,000 ft). Feel the ground rushing towards you at 200 km/h (120 mph)!

Once the parachute is deployed, take a deep breath and enjoy the 5-7 minute canopy ride which will safely take you back to the ground.Come summer or winter, we operate daily all year round (except for some national holidays).

Learn to Skydive at the most scenic dropzone! If you wish to learn how to skydive and experience the world’s best views, you've come to the right place.

  • 01

    SKYDIVE SCHOOL

     What is the AFF course? 

      The AFF training is divided into 3 levels with intensive training in both theory and practice to prepare you for all possible situations a skydiver could face. During ground school, we will cover the required emergency procedures and the fundamentals o.f piloting your canopy for a safe and accurate landing at the dropzone.

  • 02

    BASIC LEARNING

     Before Starting ... 

      Basic theoretical introduction to the aerodynamics of freefall, the opening process of the main parachute, canopy piloting techniques, landing approach, and landing techniques. To complete this level, you will do one tandem jump. This introduces you to freefall, landing pattern and how to control your body.

  • 03

    JUMP PRACTICE

     Let's give it a shot 

      You will complete three jumps accompanied by two instructors on each. The aim of these jumps is to teach you the correct exit, freefall position, basic manoeuvres of flight and altitude awareness. You will be able to open the main canopy and then steer it to land by yourself under the radio instructions from the ground instructor.

  • 04

    FINAL STEPS

     It's a Canopy time ! 

      During the next four jumps, accompanied by one instructor, you will perform a series of controlled movements (like front and back loops, turns, and rolls) to learn how to maintain a stable body position and how to recover from an unstable one. You will also learn tracking to gain horizontal separation from other skydivers before canopy deployment.

  • 05

    LIFE AFTER AFF

     Life after AFF 

      Once you have successfully completed your AFF training, you can work towards becoming a licensed skydiver. At Skydive Switzerland, we offer training for both the USPA (United States Parachute Association) license and the Swiss Skydive license (Schweizer Fallschirmspringer Lizenz).
